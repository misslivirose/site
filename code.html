Mobile Application Development

Part of my undergraduate research included building applications for the introductory mobile software course at Virginia Tech. I wrote basic applications, including a flashlight, GPS tracking, and blog reader app, and step by step tutorials for each.

Ready, Player One?

My love of VR & interest in the reach of technology's impact was kickstarted by a communications course on Games, Simulations, and Virtual Environments in Society. For the course capstone project, I designed and submitted a research proposal to the Virginia Tech IRB aimed to study gender bias in perception of gaming ability.
Java // C // C# // Python

The core of my degree in Computer Science focused around traditional software engineering practices, beginning with introductory classes in Java and going through formal languages and systems-level development in C. I also studied Python and worked primarily in C# for projects.

#Hackathons

At Hackbright Academy's 'Hack[bright] for Good' Hackathon, I worked with a team on an idea based around SMS scheduling services to propose a solution to the problem of needing connectivity in developing countries. The project also explored an approach to using the technology to build resumes via text messaging, and won first place for the use of the Twillio API. I also attended YC Hacks and used the opportunity to network and work on design skills, and built Shake!, an earthquake preparedness quiz, at the 2014 International Women's Hackathon.
Women in Tech

I participated in the Bay Area She's Geeky Unconference in January 2014 and volunteered for the Microsoft Bay Area Girl Geek dinner in March. A member of SWE in college, I have a passion for supporting diversity in STEM fields. I was a mentor & judge at the International Women's Hackathon in Phoenix as part of the 2014 Grace Hopper Conference, and occasionally write for women in engineering blogs.
The Indie Web Dev

I developed a love for CSS and HTML earlier this year and began a personal quest to complete 60 days of web development. I've built several small tools and my personal pages, and have started working on several larger web projects. You can view my past and ongoing projects on my GitHub account.
